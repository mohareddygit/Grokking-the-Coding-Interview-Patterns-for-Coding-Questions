2. Pattern-Based Learning

Instead of solving random problems, focus on mastering common "coding patterns" that solve large classes of problems:

-   **Two Pointers:**  Ideal for sorted arrays or searching for pairs.
-   **Sliding Window:**  Used for subarray or substring problems where you need to track a range of elements.
-   **BFS/DFS:**  Essential for tree and graph traversals; use BFS for shortest paths and DFS for exploring all branches.
-   **Dynamic Programming (DP):**  Used when a problem can be broken into overlapping subproblems, such as finding the "number of ways" to do something.
